Research Focus Areas

Adrenergic Receptor Regulation of Cardiac Metabolism

We explore how alpha-1A adrenergic receptors modulate mitochondrial function and metabolism in the heart, and their potential as therapeutic targets for heart failure and cardiometabolic syndrome.

Supported by NIH Grant: 2R01 HL140067

  • Postinfarct remodeling and necroptosis mitigation
  • Alpha-1A receptor regulation of mitochondrial oxidative metabolism

Cardiotoxicity of Targeted Cancer Therapies

Investigating cardiovascular side effects of kinase inhibitors and novel cancer treatments to minimize cardiotoxicity and improve patient outcomes.

Supported by NIH Grant: R01 HL165294

  • Kinome profiling of cardiotoxic kinase inhibitors
  • Metabolomics analysis of sorafenib impact on heart

Adaptive Roles of ROR-alpha in Cardiac Health

Understanding how the nuclear receptor ROR-alpha preserves mitochondrial function and mitigates cardiac hypertrophy, positioning it as a novel therapeutic target.

  • ROR-alpha regulation of caveolin-3 mediated mitophagy
  • Protection against angiotensin II-induced cardiac hypertrophy
